Welcome to a Page About Classifications of Planets!

What does it take to be classified as a planet?

According to NASA "A planet is a celestial body that (a) is in orbit around the Sun, (b) has sufficient mass for its self-gravity to overcome rigid body forces so that it assumes a hydrostatic equilibrium (nearly round) shape, and (c) has cleared the neighbourhood around its orbit."

Demotion of Pluto

On August 24th 2006, the International Astronomical Union (IAU) demoted Pluto from planet status to that of a new classification - Dwarf Planet. According to NASA "A 'dwarf planet' is a celestial body that (a) is in orbit around the Sun, (b) has sufficient mass for its self-gravity to overcome rigid body forces so that it assumes a hydrostatic equilibrium (nearly round) shape, (c) has not cleared the neighbourhood around its orbit, and (d) is not a satellite."
